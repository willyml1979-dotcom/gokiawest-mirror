Leasing vs. Buying: Understanding the Difference

Leasing and buying are two fundamentally different ways to drive a new Kia, and the right choice depends on how you use your vehicle, your financial goals, and your personal preferences. When you lease, you are essentially paying for the depreciation of the vehicle over a set term, typically 36 to 48 months, along with applicable fees and interest. At the end of the lease, you return the vehicle, purchase it at a predetermined residual value, or start a new lease on a different model.

Leasing generally results in lower monthly payments compared to financing the same vehicle, because you are not paying off the full purchase price. It also allows you to drive a newer vehicle more frequently and stay within the manufacturer's warranty period. However, leases come with annual kilometre limits, usually between 16,000 and 24,000 km per year in Canada, and excess kilometres are charged at a per-kilometre rate outlined in your contract. You are also responsible for returning the vehicle in good condition, with normal wear and tear accepted but damage beyond that subject to fees.

Buying, whether with cash or through financing, means you own the vehicle and have no restrictions on mileage or modifications. Once the loan is paid off, you have an asset with trade-in or resale value. Buying tends to make more financial sense for drivers who keep their vehicles for many years, drive high annual kilometres, or want the flexibility of full ownership.

Understanding Your Monthly Payment

Your monthly payment is determined by several factors: the vehicle price, your down payment or trade-in value, the interest rate, and the length of your loan or lease term. A larger down payment reduces the amount you need to finance, which lowers your monthly obligation and reduces the total interest you pay. Trade-in vehicles are appraised and their value can be applied directly to the purchase, functioning the same way as a cash down payment.

In British Columbia, provincial sales tax (PST) and the federal goods and services tax (GST) apply to vehicle purchases and leases, and these amounts are factored into your financing total. For new vehicles, PST in BC is 12 percent, which is an important number to account for when estimating your total cost. Our finance team will provide you with a full payment breakdown that includes all taxes, fees, and any applicable protection products before you sign anything.

We encourage customers to use our online payment calculator as a starting point, but we always recommend sitting down with one of our finance specialists to get accurate numbers based on your specific situation. Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need to get approved for financing?

There is no single minimum credit score that guarantees or prevents approval, as each lender has its own criteria. Generally speaking, a score above 650 tends to qualify for standard rates, while scores below that may still be approved through specialized lenders at higher interest rates. We work with applicants across a wide range of credit profiles and will do our best to find a solution that works for your situation.

How much should I put down on a new Kia?

There is no required minimum down payment for most financing scenarios, though putting more down reduces your monthly payment and the total interest paid over the life of the loan. A common guideline is 10 to 20 percent of the vehicle's purchase price, but many customers put down less or use a trade-in to cover part of the cost. Our finance team can show you how different down payment amounts affect your monthly payment so you can decide what makes sense for your budget.

Can I finance a vehicle if I am new to Canada or have no credit history?

Yes, we work with lenders who have programs specifically designed for newcomers to Canada and first-time buyers with limited or no credit history. In these situations, a co-signer with established Canadian credit, a larger down payment, or proof of stable employment can significantly improve your chances of approval. We encourage you to speak with one of our finance specialists directly, as the options vary and we can give you a clearer picture based on your specific circumstances.

What is the difference between a lease buyout and returning the vehicle at lease end?

At the end of your lease term, you typically have three options: return the vehicle and walk away, return it and start a new lease on a different model, or purchase the vehicle at the residual value stated in your original lease agreement. The residual value is set at the beginning of the lease and does not change, so if the vehicle has held its value well, buying it out can sometimes be a financially smart move. Our team can help you evaluate which option makes the most sense when your lease approaches its end date.

Are there kilometre restrictions if I lease a Kia?

Yes, all Kia leases in Canada come with an annual kilometre allowance, which is agreed upon at the time of signing. Standard allowances typically range from 16,000 to 24,000 km per year, though higher allowances can sometimes be negotiated upfront, usually at a slightly higher monthly payment. If you exceed your allowance at the end of the lease, you will be charged a per-kilometre fee as outlined in your contract. If you regularly drive more than 24,000 km per year, financing may be a more cost-effective choice.
