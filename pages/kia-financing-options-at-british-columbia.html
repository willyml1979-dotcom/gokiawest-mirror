Leasing vs. Buying: Understanding the Difference

Leasing and buying both have genuine advantages, and the right choice depends on how you plan to use your vehicle and what matters most to you financially. When you lease a Kia, you are essentially paying for the portion of the vehicle's value you use during the lease term — typically two to four years. Monthly lease payments are generally lower than financing payments for the same vehicle, and at the end of the term you can return the vehicle, purchase it at a predetermined residual value, or lease a new model.

Buying, on the other hand, means you are building equity in the vehicle with every payment. Once the loan is paid off, you own the car outright with no further obligations. This is often the better long-term value for drivers who keep their vehicles for many years, put high mileage on their cars, or want the freedom to modify or sell the vehicle at any time. There are no mileage restrictions when you own your vehicle, which is an important consideration for drivers who commute long distances or travel frequently within British Columbia.

Leasing tends to appeal to drivers who prefer to drive a newer vehicle every few years, want access to the latest technology and safety features, and drive a predictable number of kilometres annually. Our finance team at Go Kia West can run side-by-side comparisons of lease and purchase scenarios for any model you are considering, so you can see exactly what each option costs over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need to get approved for financing at Go Kia West?

There is no single minimum credit score required for financing. While a higher credit score generally qualifies you for better interest rates and more favorable terms, we work with a range of lenders who serve customers across the credit spectrum. Customers with scores below 600 may still be approved through our special financing programs, often with a larger down payment or co-signer. We recommend applying and letting our finance team assess your specific situation rather than assuming you will not qualify.

How much of a down payment do I need?

Down payment requirements vary depending on the lender, the vehicle, and your credit profile. For customers with strong credit, some lenders offer zero-down financing options on approved credit. For customers working through credit challenges, a larger down payment — typically 10 to 20 percent of the vehicle's purchase price — can significantly improve approval odds and reduce the overall cost of borrowing. A down payment also lowers your monthly payment and reduces the risk of being in a negative equity position.

What is the difference between a lease and a finance agreement?

A finance agreement is a loan used to purchase the vehicle. You make payments over the loan term, and at the end you own the vehicle outright. A lease is an agreement to use the vehicle for a set period and mileage allowance, after which you return it, buy it at its residual value, or start a new lease. Leases typically have lower monthly payments but come with kilometre limits and conditions around vehicle condition at return. Financing is usually better for long-term ownership, while leasing suits drivers who prefer flexibility and regular upgrades.

Can I finance a used vehicle through Go Kia West?

Yes, we offer financing on our pre-owned inventory as well as new Kia models. Interest rates on used vehicles are generally slightly higher than on new vehicles, and the available loan terms may differ depending on the age and mileage of the vehicle. Our finance team can walk you through the options available for any used vehicle in our inventory and help you determine what fits your budget.

Are there any additional products or fees included in the financing?

When finalizing your financing agreement, our finance team may present optional products such as extended warranties, paint and fabric protection, or gap insurance. These are entirely optional and will be clearly itemized so you can decide whether they make sense for your situation. Gap insurance, for example, covers the difference between what you owe on the vehicle and what your insurance pays out if the vehicle is written off — which can be valuable in the early years of a loan when depreciation is highest. We will never include products in your contract without your explicit knowledge and consent.

How long does the financing approval process take?

In most cases, we can provide a financing decision within the same business day, and often within a few hours of receiving a completed application. Submitting your application online in advance can speed up the process considerably. More complex situations — such as self-employed applicants or those with past credit issues — may take a little longer as we work with multiple lenders to find the best available option. Our finance team will keep you informed throughout the process and let you know if any additional documentation is needed.
